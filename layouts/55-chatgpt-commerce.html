What to consider

For wide feet on the trail, the toe box geometry matters more than the stated width — look for a shoe that's anatomically wide at the front rather than just a stretched upper. Rockplate protection separates true trail shoes from road crossovers. Under $120, you'll find solid options in Goretex-free versions; waterproofing adds ~$30. Stack height and drop vary a lot between brands — lower drop (<6 mm) rewards more experienced runners. Check the return window: sizing in wide trail shoes runs inconsistent.
