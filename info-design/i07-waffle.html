Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A 10×10 waffle grid — one hundred equal cells standing in for one hundred population members, filled bottom-up and left-to-right, colored by segment.
When to use it
Shares of a whole expressed as "N out of 100" — survey splits, population composition — anywhere every unit should feel countable, not just proportional.
Data shape
{label, n}[] — 3 named segments whose n values sum to at most 100; the script fills the remaining cells with an implicit "rest" group so the grid always totals 100.
Visual style
Direct labels replace a legend as a single sentence beneath the headline, each segment named inline behind a colored swatch button; the leftover cells stay flat neutral gray.

Recreate prompt
This is a waffle chart: a 10x10 grid of 100 small rounded cells stands in for a population of 100, filled from the bottom-left corner upward and left to right within each row, so it reads like a container filling rather than a chart with an axis. There is no legend box — the key is a single sentence directly under the headline that names each of the three segments inline, a small colored swatch first and its exact share of 100 second, so the reading order is: headline, then the sentence that supplies the numbers, then the grid that lets you feel the proportion at a glance. The three named segments use one accent hue at three tint strengths, boldest for the largest group and lightest for the smallest; everyone left over — the implicit fourth group the sentence doesn't need to name — fills the remaining cells in flat neutral gray. Clicking a segment's name in the key sentence briefly pulses just its cells, so a reader can trace a number they just read back into the grid on demand.
