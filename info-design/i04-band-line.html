Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A single-series line chart with a shaded confidence/range band and annotated event markers.
When to use it
KPI tracking with uncertainty — forecasts, experiment readouts, noisy metrics — where the range around the number matters as much as the number itself.
Data shape
{t, v, lo, hi}[] monthly points, plus a short list of {t, label} events — three years of history is a good default length.
Visual style
Direct year ticks instead of a dense x-axis; the band is the accent color at low opacity; two vertical event rules carry short angled labels; the endpoint is called out with its own dot and value.

This is a single line chart with a soft confidence band running behind it: the line tracks one metric across three years of monthly points, and a band in the same accent color at low opacity shows the plausible range around it at every step, so uncertainty is visible without a second series or error bars. Two vertical rules mark real-world events, each carrying a short label set at a slight angle above the line so it reads without a legend or footnote. The x-axis is stripped down to only the three year boundaries — no monthly tick clutter — and the line's final point gets its own dot and a value label in the accent color, so the current number is legible without hunting the axis. Hovering the plot snaps a crosshair and a small dot to the nearest point and opens a chip reporting that month's exact value and band range; on touch devices, where there's no real hover, the crosshair is suppressed entirely rather than showing a broken sticky state.
