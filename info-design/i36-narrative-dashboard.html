Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A large annotated area chart paired with a reading rail of four numbered notes, cross-highlighted in both directions.
When to use it
Post-mortems, monthly narratives, board packs — anywhere the reader needs the exact moments a trend changed, explained in prose, not just plotted.
Data shape
One 24-point main series plus exactly four annotations — {n, title, text, target:{x0,x1}}[] — where target names the x-range each note is about.
Visual style
A soft accent band marks each note's x-range behind the line; a small numbered marker sits on the line at each band's midpoint; the rail's number badge and background respond the moment its band or marker is hovered, and vice versa.

This is a narrative dashboard: a large annotated area chart on the left carries the whole story, and a reading rail of four numbered notes on the right explains it, so the page works as a short memo rather than a control panel. Four soft vertical bands sit behind the line, each spanning the exact months one note is about, and a small numbered marker sits directly on the line at the middle of its band — so every note has a literal, findable home on the chart rather than a footnote reference. The cross-highlight runs both ways: hovering a note tints its band and lights up its marker, and hovering a marker or its band brings the matching note forward in the rail with an accent-filled number and a tinted background, so a reader can work from the story to the chart or from the chart to the story with the same result either way. Only two gridlines and five month ticks keep the chart quiet enough for the bands and markers to carry the eye instead of axis clutter.
