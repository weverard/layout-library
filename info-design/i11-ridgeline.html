Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A ridgeline (joyplot) — five overlapping density curves, one per group, stacked with a shared baseline offset so their shapes can be compared at a glance.
When to use it
Comparing the shape of a distribution — not just its average — across several groups: checkout time by lane, response time by team, activity by hour.
Data shape
{label, pts, focal}[] — 5 groups x 24 precomputed density heights (0-1) each; each row's median tick is derived from its curve at render time, not stored or estimated from raw samples.
Visual style
One --accent-filled row for the focal group, translucent ink for the rest; row names sit directly on their own baseline instead of a shared legend.
