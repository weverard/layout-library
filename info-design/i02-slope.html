Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A slope chart: two vertical baselines, one per time point, joined by a straight line per entity.
When to use it
Before/after comparisons across enough entities that a grouped-bar chart would get crowded — rank shifts, share changes, gains and losses at a glance.
Data shape
{label, then, now}[] — works well from about 5 up to 10-12 entities before lines start to overlap.
Visual style
Direct end labels instead of a legend; steepening or shallowing slope is the whole story; risers and fallers split by color, and one focal entity gets a heavier line and a note.

Recreate prompt
This is a slope chart: two vertical baselines stand for two moments in time, and one straight line per entity connects its value at each moment, so the slope itself — not a bar, not a bubble — carries the whole story. Every line ends in a small dot with its label and exact value sitting right beside it, so nothing needs a legend; the eye reads the identity and the number from the same spot as the shape. Lines that rose are drawn in the accent color, lines that fell stay a neutral warm gray, splitting the field into two readable groups at a glance. One entity is marked as the focal point: its line runs heavier than the rest and carries a short annotation, so the single most important shift is impossible to miss even in a crowd of six lines. Hovering any line or its label brings that entity forward at full strength and fades the other five back, turning a busy chart into an on-demand single-series view.
