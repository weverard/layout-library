Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A GitHub-style year grid: columns are weeks, rows are days, and each cell's fill steps through a 5-level sequential ramp by value.
When to use it
Any single daily metric over a year where the reader cares about rhythm and streaks more than exact numbers — commits, workouts, logins, support tickets opened.
Data shape
{date, value}[364] — 52 weeks x 7 days; here generated by a seeded pseudo-random function so the pattern is stable across loads, not authored by hand.
Visual style
Five accent-opacity steps from the neutral gridline color to full accent; month initials run along the top, weekday initials down the left; a dashed ring calls out the single densest run of high days, found by scanning the data rather than placed by eye.

This is a calendar heatmap in the GitHub-contributions style: a wide grid of small square cells, one column per week and one row per day of the week, running left to right across a full year. Each cell's fill steps through five discrete levels of one accent color, from a near-invisible neutral tint at the lowest level to fully saturated at the highest, so the eye reads a texture of rhythm rather than individual numbers. Month abbreviations sit in a thin row above the grid, each one appearing once at the column where that month begins; single-letter weekday initials run down the left edge. A five-swatch legend below the grid is labeled "less" on one end and "more" on the other, standing in for a value axis. One short vertical run of consecutive high-level cells is circled with a thin dashed ring and a small note naming its length, computed by scanning the data for the longest run of top-tier days rather than placed by eye. Hovering any cell shows a small dark chip with its exact date and value. On narrow screens the grid does not shrink or squash its cells — it sits in a fixed-width track that scrolls horizontally instead, so every cell stays a legible, equal size.
