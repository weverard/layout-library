Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A streamgraph — five series stacked as smooth wiggle bands around a shifting center baseline, so the whole composition's total width breathes with the data instead of sitting on a flat axis.
When to use it
Composition that evolves over time — channel mix, content-format mix, any "share of the whole" story across enough time points that a stacked bar chart would flicker.
Data shape
5 series x 12 points; the script derives stacked {y0, y1} offsets from each series' raw monthly values, centering the stack so the baseline itself wiggles.
Visual style
A muted five-step tint ramp derived from one accent hue stands in for a legend; each series is labeled directly inside the column where its band is widest.

This is a streamgraph: five series stack into smooth, bezier-curved bands whose combined width breathes wider and narrower across twelve monthly columns, centered on a baseline that wiggles instead of sitting flat at zero, which is what separates it from an ordinary stacked area chart. There is no legend — each band is filled with one step of a muted tint ramp built from a single accent hue, and its name is set directly inside the column where that band happens to be widest, so the label always lands somewhere legible. Hovering any band saturates it and fades the other four back, and a chip follows the cursor reporting that series' name and its percentage share of the total in whichever month sits under the pointer. Below roughly 520px the wiggle collapses to a conventional flat-baseline stacked area with straight segments instead of curves, trading a little visual interest for legibility at a small size.
