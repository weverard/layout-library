    Spec — what it is, when to use it
    What it is
    A scatter plot of 30 accounts with a precomputed linear regression line; the three points that depart most from the fit switch to accent fill and are called out by name.
    When to use it
    Any time you want to relate two continuous measures and are prepared to name the handful of points that don't fit the pattern, rather than letting them sit unexplained in the cloud.
    Data shape
    {x, y, label}[] plus a precomputed regression {m, b, r2} — the fit is computed once offline and hardcoded, never recomputed in the browser.
    Visual style
    Ink dots on a hairline horizontal grid; a single accent regression line carries its equation and R² quietly at its own trailing end; three outliers get accent badges with curved leader lines to a name and a one-line reason.

    Recreate prompt
    This is a scatter plot: thirty small ink-colored dots at reduced opacity, plotted against a single hairline horizontal grid with no vertical gridlines at all. A single accent-colored regression line runs through the cloud, and instead of a caption or legend box, its equation and R² value are set quietly in small monospace type right at the line's own trailing end, so the statistic reads as part of the line rather than a separate callout. Three points that sit furthest from the fit switch from ink to the accent color and gain a small numbered badge; a thin dashed line curves outward from each badge to a short two-line label giving the account's name and, below it in muted gray, the one-sentence reason it's worth naming. Hovering any dot reveals a small dark tooltip with its exact values. On narrow screens the curved leader lines and their labels disappear entirely and the same three explanations reappear as a plain numbered list beneath the chart, so the reasoning survives even when there isn't room to point at it directly.
