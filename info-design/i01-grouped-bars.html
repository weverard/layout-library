Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A grouped (paired) vertical bar chart: each category shows two side-by-side bars, one per series.
When to use it
Comparing two measures across a handful of categories — plan vs. actual, this period vs. last, us vs. benchmark.
Data shape
{label, a, b}[] — 3-6 categories keeps groups legible; more than that, consider small multiples instead.
Visual style
Direct labels replace a legend; one hairline-gridline y-axis with top-hung labels; a single annotated bar carries the takeaway.

Recreate prompt
This is a grouped bar chart comparing two series across four categories, drawn as thin vertical bar pairs with generous whitespace between groups. The accent color marks the primary series (this quarter); the second series stays a neutral warm gray so it reads as context, not competition. There is no legend box — instead, the two series are named directly above the bars in the first group only, so the key is learned once and then read by color for the rest of the chart. Three hairline horizontal gridlines mark the y-axis, each with its value hung just above the line at the plot's left edge rather than stacked in a vertical axis column. The one bar that breaks the pattern carries a thin callout line up to a short, specific sentence of annotation, so the headline insight is pointed at directly rather than left for the reader to spot in the shape of the bars.
