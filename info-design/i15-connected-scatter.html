Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A connected scatter plot: one entity's yearly points joined point-to-point in year order, with small arrowheads carrying the direction of travel.
When to use it
Two metrics that move together over time in a way a simple line chart can't show — because the path can double back on one axis while still advancing on the other.
Data shape
{year, x, y}[] — one entity, 12 yearly points, in chronological order.
Visual style
A faint gray ghost of the path sits just behind the accent path for depth; the first and last points are emphasized and year-labeled, the inflection year gets its own callout, and mid-segment arrowheads make the direction of travel legible without a separate legend.

Recreate prompt
This is a connected scatter plot: twelve yearly points for a single entity, joined point-to-point by a smooth accent-colored path that loops rather than running straight, since the two metrics don't move in lockstep. A faint gray duplicate of the same path sits just behind it, offset by a few pixels, giving the line quiet depth without a drop shadow. Small triangular arrowheads sit at the midpoint of each segment, rotated to point along the direction of travel, so the reader can tell which way time runs even where the path bends back on itself. Most points are small open rings in the accent color; the first and last points are solid and larger, each with its year set directly beside it in bold, and the one year where the relationship visibly changes gets its own short callout note rather than being left for the reader to spot in the bend of the line. Hovering any point reveals a small dark tooltip with the year and both exact values. On narrow screens the arrowheads disappear and only every third year keeps its label, so the chart doesn't fill with overlapping text.
