Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A dumbbell (dot-range) chart: two dots per row, joined by a track, showing a from-value and a to-value.
When to use it
Gap analysis and before/after comparisons across a group of items, sorted so the biggest movers lead.
Data shape
{label, from, to}[], sorted by delta — 6-10 rows keeps the ranking readable.
Visual style
Open vs. filled dot stands in for before/after (stated once in a key line, not a legend box); the delta is labeled at the row's end, and the largest one is annotated.

This is a dumbbell chart: horizontal rows, each holding two dots on a shared track, one for a before-value and one for an after-value, sorted so the row with the biggest gap sits at the top and the rest fall off in order. Instead of a legend box, an open dot for "before" and a filled, accent-colored dot for "after" are explained once in a short key sentence beneath the dek, then read by shape and fill for every row after that. Each row's exact gap is labeled in the accent color at the end of its track, so the ranking reads as a column of numbers as well as a column of shapes. The row with the largest gap gets a short annotation pointing at it directly, naming the headline finding instead of leaving the reader to spot the longest track. Hovering a row lifts a soft band behind it and reveals the two precise values beside their dots.
