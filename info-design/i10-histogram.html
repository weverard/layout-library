Spec — what it is, when to use it
What it is
A histogram — roughly 200 placeholder values pre-binned into 20 equal-width buckets — with dashed reference lines marking the mean and median, and a row of stat chips summarizing n, mean, median, and p90.
When to use it
Any single numeric measure where the shape of the spread matters more than any one summary number — response times, prices, test scores, wait times.
Data shape
{bins:number[20], binWidth, min, unit, stats:{n,mean,median,p90}} — bin counts and summary stats are computed once offline and hardcoded; nothing is binned or aggregated at runtime.
Visual style
Hairline gaps between accent-colored bars stand in for an axis; two dashed vertical lines carry small dot-and-label "flags" for mean and median so the reader doesn't have to eyeball them off the bars; a short note calls the skewed tail out directly.
